Lasagne Recipe

Hugh Fearnley Whittingstall's Lasagne

The secret to a great lasagne is quality of the sauce, and the key to that is coarse ground beef and long, slow cooking. Serves six to eight.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 good knob of butter
  • 1 medium oniom, finely chopped
  • 1 stick celery, finely diced
  • 1 large carrot, finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 125g streaky bacon, finely diced
  • 750g coarsely ground beef
  • 100g free-range chicken liver, chopped fine (optional)
  • Salt and ground black pepper
  • 250ml dry whole milk
  • A couple of grating of nutmeg
  • 250ml dry white wine
  • 400g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 500ml beef stock
  • 2 bay leaves

For the béchamel:

  • 750ml hot milk, infused with a bay leaf and a slice of onion
  • 50g of butter
  • 50g of plain flower
  • A grind or two of nutmeg
  • Salt and ground black pepper
  • 10-14 Lasagne sheets (epending on size of dish), fresh or dried
  • 60g parmesan grated
  • 20g butter

Method

  1. First make he meat sauce. Warm the oil and butter in a large saucepan over a medium-low heat. Add the onion, celery and carrot, and sweat until soft, about 10 mins. Add the garlic, bacon, beef, liver and a large pinch of salt and brown the meat. Add the milk and simmer until the wine evaporates.

  2. Stir in the tomatoes, stock and bay leaves, bring to a simmer, then turn down the heat so its barely bubbling. Cook, ucover, for three hours (yes, really), stirring from time to time. Add a little stock or water if it looks as if it's drying to out too much - if using dried pasta, keep the sauce a little more liquid.

  3. Preheat the oven to 180c/350f/gas mark4. To make the béchamel, pour the milk into a pan, add the bay leaf and warm gently while you make a rouc in another pan. Melt the butter over a low heat, add the flour and stir for a few minutes. Add a quarter of the hot milk, whisk in to the roux, the slowly add the rest of the milk. Whisking all the time to ensure there are no lumps. turn the heat down to low and simmer for 5 minutes, stirring frequently. Add half the parmesan and season with nutmeg, salt and pepper.

  4. To assemble, spoon a quarter of the béchamel in the base of an ovenproof dish followed by a layer of bolognese and a layer of pasta. Repeat, finishing with the last quarter of béchamel on top of the third layer of pasta. Sprinkle over the remaining Parmesan and dot with butter. Bake for 25 minutes, until golden and bubbling. Allow to cool slightly before serving.
